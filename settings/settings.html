Water Level

The water level controls the inches of water your landscape will receive per week. High is 2 inches per week, medium is 1 inch per week, and low is 0.5 inches per week. Additionally there is a Custom option to choose among two more amounts. The default setting is 1 inch per week.

Warning! Changing the water level in the middle of the week changes the level for the entire week, including past days, to the new chosen level. Any water your lawn received in the beginning of the week is subtracted from the new value. If the result is below 0" your lawn will not receive any more water for the current week.

Water Rate

This controls the sprinkler system's water output rate used in watering time calculations. The default is 1 inch per hour. So if you have the water level on medium (1 inch per week) and use the default water rate of 1 inch per hour, your lawn will receive 1 hour of watering throughout the week if no precipitation occurs.

Watering Time

This controls the time that your sprinklers will water on a given day. Generally the best time to water is between 4 am and 10 am. Watering too early in the morning causes fungus to grow, while watering in the middle of the day causes the water to evaporate too quickly. If you cannot water in the morning for some reason then you should water between 4 pm and 7 pm. The default time is 7 am.

What is the progress bar on the homepage?

The progress bar allows you to see how much water your lawn has got for the week compared to the amount needed. The blue portion represents the amount that has been watered by the sprinkler system, while the green portion represents the amount of rain your lawn has received for the current week. The progress bar fills up either when your lawn has met or exceeded the water level goal for the week.

How much water does my lawn need per week?

While different types of lawns need different amounts of water, in general lawns need at least 1 inch per week. This web-page lists some good tips.

How do I know my sprinkler's water rate?

While the default setting is 1 inch per hour, you may want to get a more accurate number to allow for our program to water your landscape properly. You can easily find your sprinkler's water rate by placing at least two water gauges or cans around your lawn the next time you water. Keep track of the amount of time your lawn is watered, and enter the average of the water amount between the cans below to obtain your water rate. In settings select the water rate that is closest to the below value.

I updated my settings/events on one device, why haven't they changed on my other device?

ADEM caches your settings and events in the browser, and does not pull updates from another device on the Settings or Schedule page. You will have to go to the Home page to pull updates from another device. This technique allows the settings and schedule page to load much faster.
